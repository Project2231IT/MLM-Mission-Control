**REGISTER RECONCILIATION**

*AI can automate comparison, flag discrepancies, and generate reports.*

| 5\. | Walk me through exactly what you compare at end of day. What numbers, from where, matched against what? |
| :---- | :---- |
|  |  **The “expected totals" are the sales according to Go Antiquing. We compare these to our "actual totals” which is our full cash drawer, our closed credit card batch (from credit card machine), and our closed gift card batch (from credit card machine).  This is done for each individual register and then an “all registers” sheet is printed and compared.**  |

| 6\. | How long does the full process take? |
| :---- | :---- |
|  |  **25-45 minutes.**   |

| 7\. | When there is a discrepancy, what do you do? Who do you tell, and how do you document it? |
| :---- | :---- |
|  |  **If there is a discrepancy on either gift card/credit card, we must go through every receipt from the corresponding register. We staple the Go Antiquing receipt to the credit card/gift card receipt from the card terminal. But, if a receipt is lost, there is not a way to correctly identify which transaction is incorrect.   If there is a cash discrepancy, there is no way to identify when/who shorted the drawer. \*Discrepancies occur because our card terminals do not sync with our point of sales. My team must see the sales total on the Go Antiquing screen and then hand type it into the card terminal. This creates a margin of error that causes frequent discrepancies.**   |

| 8\. | Where does the final result go? A form, a spreadsheet, a piece of paper, an email? |
| :---- | :---- |
|  |  **The final result goes on a paper copy of the all registers close out sheet. This is then picked up by accounting.  Once accounting has it, I am not sure if they create a digital copy.**   |
